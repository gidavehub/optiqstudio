## 4.1 THE CORE PROBLEM: THE MODEL'S DEFAULT AFRICA

Ask any video model for "a Gambian market" and it will give you a **beautiful lie**: impossibly saturated, suspiciously clean, lit like a perfume ad, populated by models in costume-department "African print," set against a savanna that looks like a screensaver. It is the Africa of stock libraries and charity appeals. It is instantly, viscerally fake to anyone who has been there â€” and it is *especially* fake to the Gambian audience we are selling to.

**Every unspecified element defaults to this.** The engine below is how we overwrite it.

## 4.2 THE SPECIFICITY LADDER

**RUNG 0 â€” The label.** `"a Gambian market"` â†’ **fake**. This is where the default lives.

## 4.3 THE GAMBIAN DETAIL LIBRARY

The agent's ammunition. Deploy liberally; these are the words that buy authenticity.

### TRANSPORT
- **gele-gele** â€” battered yellow-and-green minibus, dented old Mercedes van, packed solid, roof rack with lashed sacks
- **the apprentice** â€” lean young man in a faded football jersey and plastic sandals, hanging out of the sliding door, gripping the frame, a fold of grimy dalasi notes in his fist, calling the destination
- **the flag-down** â€” stand on the left of the road, body parallel to traffic, right arm extended slightly into the road, arm held still, **open palm flapping up and down**. This is *the* gesture. Never "waves down a bus."
- green-and-yellow taxis; a scooter that toots and swerves; a handcart; a wheelbarrow of sacks

### THE STREET & THE COMPOUND
- packed-earth and patched-tarmac roads; red laterite shoulders; crumbling verges
- painted concrete perimeter walls, faded ochre/cream; worn metal gates (they *clang*)
- corrugated zinc roofs (they shimmer in heat); breeze-block; open gutters
- electricity poles with tangled wires; washing lines; a stray goat; swept-earth yards
- **bitik** â€” small concrete roadside kiosk, hand-painted with faded soft-drink and telecom colours, sachets of water hanging in a plastic bag from a nail, a wooden bench outside
- **attaya corner** â€” men on a bench around a small charcoal stove with tiny glasses

### THE MARKET
- wooden-and-zinc stalls, faded tarpaulin/fabric awnings, jute sacks, umbrella stands
- white plastic monobloc chairs, often mismatched, stamped with faded soft-drink branding
- wide enamel basins; woven baskets; a hand-held scale; sacks of rice with tops rolled down
- hanging bundles of dried fish; baskets of chillies and tomatoes; pyramids of oranges
- women carrying wide basins **on their heads**, one hand steadying
- porters with huge loads on their heads, bellowing warnings

### THE HOME
- outdoor kitchen: large **blackened aluminium pot**, soot-darkened, over a **wood fire** or a **coal pot** (three-stone or metal)
- a long **wooden stirring spoon**; a plastic kettle; worn aluminium and plastic bowls
- interiors: bare or worn cement/lino floors, plain plastered walls, very little furniture, **spotless**
- **a single candle melted onto a saucer** â€” for poverty, this beats "a dim bulb or lantern" every time. It is the true detail.
- a thin foam mattress on a low frame; a folded printed cloth for a blanket
- eating: the family rings **one large shared bowl** on a **woven mat on the floor**, eating with the **right hand**; an elder nudges meat toward a child's side of the bowl

### DRESS
- **wax-print** wrapper-and-blouse; **grand mbubb** (women's flowing gown); **musor** (tall sculpted headwrap)
- **grand boubou** / embroidered **kaftan** + **kufi cap** (men)
- everyday: faded t-shirts, football jerseys (often oversized), rubber flip-flops, plastic slippers
- for occasions: brilliant wax-print and lace, gold hoops, amber/beaded necklaces

### FOOD
- **domoda** â€” thick brown groundnut sauce over rice. *The* signature dish. The reason groundnut paste exists.
- **benachin**; **tapalapa** bread; **attaya** (mint tea, in tiny glasses)
- **deygeh / tigadege** â€” groundnut paste

### LANDSCAPE (handle with care â€” see Â§4.4)

- mango trees (dense shade), neem, flamboyant, cashew, baobab, acacia

### SOUND

## 4.4 ANTI-FAKE CLAUSES

Bolt these onto the STYLE or SETTING blocks when the model reaches for the postcard.

**The photoreal escalation (deploy when a wide shot reads as CGI):**

```

**The anti-pretty clause (for working scenes):**
```

**The anti-jolly clause (for working people):**

**The mess clause:**
```

```

## 4.5 THE PHOTOREAL ESCALATION

A hard-won production lesson, stated as a rule:

> **When a wide landscape reads as fake, don't fight it with adjectives. Change the location to somewhere the model has real footage of.**

Epic empty savanna, dramatic vistas, and sweeping natural landscapes are where AI models betray themselves most â€” there's less real reference data, so they hallucinate a screensaver. **Neighbourhoods, streets, markets, rooms and compounds are dense with real reference and render photoreal far more reliably.**

So: when the DEX rider's golden savanna wide looked fake, the fix wasn't more prompt-craft on the savanna. The fix was: **put him in a real neighbourhood at dawn instead.** Same beauty, same scale, but built from textures the model actually knows.

**Corollary:** favour **specific human environments** over **empty natural grandeur**. It's more authentic, more local, more on-brand, and it renders better. The epic is in the *life*, not the vista.
